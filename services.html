Cause and effects of ozone layer depletion.

Cause of ozone layer depletion

The primary reason for ozone layer depletion is the introduction of man-made chemicals, such as chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), into the Earth's atmosphere. These chemicals react with ozone molecules, breaking them down, and causing a hole to form in the ozone layer above Antarctica.

The role of human activity

The ozone layer is threatened by human-made substances that release ozone-depleting chemicals into the atmosphere. These chemicals, such as chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), are responsible for the depletion of the ozone layer. Therefore, it is crucial for humans to take action to reduce their use of such chemicals to protect the ozone layer.

Ozone Depletion Effects on Environment and Health

The depletion of the ozone layer leads to an increase in the amount of UV radiation that reaches the Earth's surface. This can have harmful effects on both the environment and human health, including skin cancer, cataracts, and immune deficiency disorders.

Ongoing Challenges and Threats

1987

In 1987, the Montreal Protocol was ratified by every country globally to stop producing CFCs that damage the ozone layer.

1992

The UN Framework on Climate Change (UNFCC) was established in 1992, founding the annual forum - the Conference of the Parties (COP).

2005

2005, the Kyoto Protocol became legally binding, requiring signatories to reduce their carbon dioxide emissions by at least 4.7%.

2015

In 2015, the Paris Agreement aimed to keep the rise in average global temperature to under 2oC and preferably to under 1.5oC above pre-industrial levels.
